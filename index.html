// Assumptions
Each transaction has a unique ID shared across platform and bank.
Currency is recorded; FX conversion is out of scope.
Settlement window is the same calendar month (Mar 2024).
Amounts are compared with ±0.01 tolerance for rounding.
A negative bank amount signals a reversal or refund.
Data is clean enough that txn_id collision = genuine duplicate.
// To Explain It To A 5 y/o
Imagine you sell lemonade and write down every cup you sell in your notebook. At the end of the day your mom checks the money jar. Sometimes the jar has less money than your notebook says — maybe you forgot to write one down, or someone gave you the wrong coins, or you wrote the same sale twice by mistake. LedgerLens compares your notebook with the money jar and tells you exactly which pages don't match — and why.
